why we built this

Dating didn't get harder.
It got shallower.

Somewhere along the way, finding a partner became a numbers game. Swipe fast. Match often. Ghost freely. Move on. The apps optimized for engagement — and in doing so, they optimized against commitment.

The people who suffer most aren't the casual daters... The people who suffer most are the ones who genuinely want something real. The ones who show up, invest, open up — only to find the other person was never really there.

bonnie was built to fix the mentality. Not the algorithm. The mentality. The belief that partnership is something you stumble into rather than something you pursue with intention, preparation, and conviction.

Every feature in bonnie exists to answer one question: does this person actually mean it?

Dating apps reward volume over depth. The more you swipe, the more they earn.
the consequence
Serious people burn out. They stop believing the right person is out there.
the fix

Everything you
want to know.

Is bonnie really free for women?+
Yes — always, with no asterisks. Women never pay to join, build a profile, receive pursuits, join a call, or connect. The cost is entirely on the men pursuing. This is intentional: he's pursuing, he invests. You never had to pay to be worth finding.
What happens after I join the waitlist?+
You'll get an email confirming your spot. When bonnie opens in Atlanta, waitlist members get first access before the app goes public. We'll reach out with instructions on how to build your profile or start your application depending on whether you're joining as a man or a woman.
Why does a man have to film videos every week?+
Because a man who shows up every week — who re-films, re-commits, keeps his profile current — is already demonstrating the consistency that matters in a real relationship. The weekly ritual is also anti-catfishing by design: no uploaded photos, no recycled content, live camera only. She sees who he is right now, not who he was two years ago.
What if the video call goes well but I'm not ready to decide?+
You have 24 hours after the call to decide whether to connect. There's no pressure to decide in the moment. Sleep on it. If you don't respond within 24 hours, the match closes — for both of you. Decisions made from clarity matter more than ones made from anxiety.
How do I know the men on bonnie are actually who they say they are?+
Every man on bonnie goes through identity verification — real name, real photo, real age — before his application is reviewed. He then films 9 videos live from his camera every single week. No uploads from a gallery, no recycled photos, no old footage. What you're watching was filmed this week. On top of that, every application is reviewed by a real person before access is granted, and a three-strike pledge system removes men who don't show up as promised. By the time a man reaches your profile, he has been verified, reviewed, and held accountable. No other app can say that.
What is the pledge and what happens if someone breaks it?+
Every member signs a pledge at onboarding. It covers responding within 48 hours, showing up to calls, and proposing a real date as the first message after connecting. Three violations result in a permanent account review. Women can report in one tap and are always notified of the outcome.
Why Atlanta first?+
Atlanta has a thriving community of intentional, accomplished, partnership-minded people who have been failed by every app built for someone else. It's dense enough for matching to work well, and the culture of the city — its ambition, its community, its standards — fits bonnie's philosophy better than anywhere else. Atlanta goes first because Atlanta deserves to.
What does "the app wants you to leave" mean?+
Every other dating app profits from you staying single — the moment you find someone, you stop paying. bonnie is built around the opposite incentive. When you connect with someone real, bonnie actively prompts you to delete the app. We measure success by how many people stop needing us. That's the whole point.
